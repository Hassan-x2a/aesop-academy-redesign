# Expert Challenge: AI Orientation
## Leadership-Tier Certification Test (REVISED)

**Based on Tier 1 Course Content:** AI Orientation chapters 1-6

---

## Rubric Dimensions (Plain Language)

You'll be evaluated on:

1. **Conceptual accuracy** — Do you understand what AI is and is not, with precision?
2. **Vocabulary fluency** — Can you use Tier 1 terms (artificial intelligence, machine learning, generative AI, model, chatbot, assistant, prompt, response, token, context, training data, inference, hallucination, multimodal, privacy) accurately?
3. **Applied judgment** — Can you apply AI concepts to real leadership decisions (when to use AI, when not to, what risks to manage)?
4. **Evidence quality** — Can you support your reasoning with examples from your experience?
5. **Reasoning defense** — Can you explain your thinking and acknowledge limitations?
6. **Risk awareness** — Can you identify hallucination risk, privacy concerns, and capability limits?
7. **Practical wisdom** — Does your reasoning reflect real-world constraints and trade-offs?

---

## Phase 1: Diagnostic Question

**What is the single most important distinction between what AI is and what it is not?**

Use at least two of these terms correctly in your answer:
- Artificial intelligence
- Machine learning
- Generative AI
- Learned patterns (from training data)

Show your thinking. Don't worry about perfection—I'm listening for whether you can separate hype from actual capability.

---

## Phase 2: Models and Applications

**A company uses an AI assistant to answer customer questions about product features. The assistant was trained on product documentation from 2023 and is updated monthly with new docs.**

1. What is the AI doing, and what can it reliably do well?

2. What are the limitations and risks? (Hint: Think about training data, context, hallucination.)

3. A customer asks about a product feature released in June 2024. The assistant confidently describes it. What just happened, and why should the company care?

---

## Phase 3: Prompts, Context, and Reliability

**Your organization wants to use an AI chatbot to handle employee onboarding questions.**

1. Design a system prompt (role and rules) that would make the assistant more reliable and safer.

2. What information would you ground the assistant with, and why?

3. What questions should employees be trained to ask directly to humans instead?

---

## Phase 4: Privacy, Data, and Organizational Risk

**A team wants to use an AI assistant to analyze internal customer data (names, emails, purchase history, account notes) to find patterns.**

1. What are the privacy risks?

2. What questions should the leadership team ask before proceeding?

3. Name one specific safeguard and explain why it matters.

---

## Phase 5: Leadership Reasoning Under Challenge

**You're advising the board on AI adoption. Here's the pressure:**

*"AI is transforming every company. We're falling behind. We should deploy AI tools to every department immediately."*

Using what you've learned about AI capabilities and limitations:

1. What is this argument missing?

2. What would a responsible rollout plan look like?

3. Name one misconception about AI that this argument reflects.

---

## Final Assessment: Judgment and Wisdom

**You encounter two claims:**

**Claim A:** "Our AI system is unbiased because it was trained on objective data."

**Claim B:** "AI can hallucinate confidently. We need humans to verify every output for factual accuracy."

1. Evaluate each claim. Which is closer to the truth? Why?

2. How would you explain hallucination to a non-technical executive?

3. What process would you put in place to catch hallucinations before they reach customers?

---

## Certification Standard

You earn Leadership-tier certification if you demonstrate:

- ✓ Accurate understanding of AI capabilities and limits (not hype)
- ✓ Correct use of Tier 1 vocabulary
- ✓ Leadership judgment: thoughtful trade-offs, risk-aware decisions
- ✓ Acknowledgment of what AI cannot reliably do
- ✓ Practical wisdom: solutions that work in real organizations
