## Executive Summary

This document details the alignment of AESOP Academy's 28-skill taxonomy with two major external frameworks:

1. **O*NET OnLine** (U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics) — occupational classification system with 900+ occupations
2. **WEF Future of Jobs Skills** (World Economic Forum, 2024-2025) — skills critical for the future workforce

All 28 AESOP skills have been mapped to:
- **O*NET occupational codes** (e.g., 15-2051.00 for Data Scientists)
- **WEF skill categories** (e.g., Critical Thinking, Analytical Thinking, Creativity)
- **Job zone classifications** (1-5 scale, 5 = highest education required)
- **Future importance rankings** (2024-2028 labor market outlook)

**Key Finding:** AESOP's taxonomy aligns well with both frameworks, ensuring that learner skill mastery is recognized by employers and institutions following industry-standard classifications.

## 1. O*NET OnLine Framework

### 1.1 Overview

**Source:** [O*NET Resource Center](https://www.onetcenter.org/)

O*NET OnLine is the primary occupational database of the U.S. Department of Labor, providing comprehensive information about:
- 900+ occupations across all industries
- Occupational codes (SOC — Standard Occupational Classification)
- Job tasks and work activities
- Required knowledge, skills, and abilities
- Education and training requirements
- Wages and employment trends

**Data Update:** The O*NET database is continuously updated. Version 30.2 (2024) includes:
- Updated Job Zone assignments for 325 occupations
- 171 "Hot Technologies" with 11,500+ occupation linkages
- 2,400+ "In Demand" skill designations

### 1.3 Job Zone Classifications

**Job Zones** represent the typical education and experience level required:

- **Zone 1:** High school graduate or equivalent; little/no experience
- **Zone 2:** Some high school or on-the-job training
- **Zone 3:** High school graduate or equivalent with training/experience
- **Zone 4:** Bachelor's degree or higher; specialized training
- **Zone 5:** Master's degree or higher; significant experience

**AESOP Alignment:**
- **Novice/Developing skills** typically correspond to **Job Zones 2-3** (high school + some training)
- **Proficient skills** typically correspond to **Job Zone 3-4** (high school+ or bachelor's)
- **Advanced skills** typically correspond to **Job Zone 4-5** (bachelor's or higher)

### 1.4 O*NET Work Activities Mapped to AESOP Skills

**Example:** Data Scientists (15-2051.00)

Relevant work activities from O*NET:
- "Determine AI and machine learning training data requirements, ensuring model validity"
- "Develop machine learning models using statistical techniques and programming languages"
- "Test machine learning models and validate results against established standards"
- "Communicate complex technical findings to non-technical stakeholders"

**AESOP skills addressing these activities:**
- `understand-ai-basics`, `grasp-llm-behavior`, `recognize-ai-limitations` (understanding models)
- `collect-and-prepare-data`, `perform-data-analysis`, `build-predictive-models` (data and ML)
- `secure-ai-systems`, `detect-ai-vulnerabilities` (validation and safety)
- `communicate-ai-concepts` (stakeholder communication)

### 1.5 Key Findings from O*NET Analysis

1. **AI/ML skills embedded in many occupations:** O*NET shows "AI and machine learning" as an "In Demand" technology across 495+ occupations, not just data science roles.

2. **Security and safety increasingly critical:** Information Security (15-1212.00) and Systems roles emphasize AI security as demand grows.

3. **Soft skills matter:** Communication (15-1254.00, Web Developers), leadership (11-3021.00), and adaptability appear across job descriptions.

4. **Emerging "Hot Technologies":**
   - Machine Learning / AI
   - Cloud Computing
   - Cybersecurity
   - Data Analysis
   - Prompt Engineering (newly prominent in 2024 updates)

5. **Job Zone progression:** AESOP learners progressing from Novice → Proficient → Advanced roughly track Job Zone 2 → 3 → 4-5, making skill progression realistic for career advancement.

## 2. WEF Future of Jobs Skills Framework

### 2.1 Overview

**Source:** [World Economic Forum Future of Jobs Report 2025](https://www.weforum.org/publications/the-future-of-jobs-report-2025/)

The WEF Future of Jobs Report surveys 1,000+ leading global employers (14 million workers) across 55 economies to identify:
- Skills most critical for 2024-2028
- Skills gaining importance fastest
- Emerging skill categories
- Sector-specific skill demand

**Key Insight:** Employers expect 39% of key skills to change by 2030, requiring workforce reskilling and lifelong learning.

### 2.2 WEF Skill Categories (2024-2025)

The WEF identifies skills across five domains:

#### **1. Technology Skills**
- AI and machine learning
- Big data and analytics
- Cloud computing
- Cybersecurity and networks
- Technological literacy

#### **2. Cognitive Skills**
- Critical thinking
- Analytical thinking
- Creative thinking
- Complex problem-solving
- Learning and adaptability

#### **3. Self-Efficacy Skills**
- Resilience and flexibility
- Curiosity and lifelong learning
- Initiative and self-direction
- Awareness and growth mindset

#### **4. Management Skills**
- Leadership and social influence
- Talent management and development
- Change management
- Decision-making

#### **5. Working with Others**
- Communication and collaboration
- Teamwork
- Social and emotional intelligence
- Conflict resolution

### 2.3 Top 10 Skills for 2024-2028

According to WEF's 2025 report:

1. **Critical Thinking**
2. **Analytical Thinking**
3. **Creativity**
4. **Resilience & Flexibility**
5. **Curiosity & Lifelong Learning**
6. **AI and Big Data**
7. **Technological Literacy**
8. **Leadership & Social Influence**

10. **Environmental Stewardship & Sustainability**

## 3. Integration & Validation

### 3.1 Cross-Framework Validation

**Process:**
1. Each AESOP skill mapped independently to O*NET codes and WEF categories
2. Overlaps identified (e.g., both frameworks emphasize critical thinking in AI roles)
3. Gaps examined (e.g., emerging skills not yet in O*NET)

**Result:** Strong agreement between frameworks on:
- Importance of foundational AI understanding
- Criticality of security and responsible AI
- Rising importance of prompt engineering and LLM understanding
- Need for cross-functional skills (communication, ethics, problem-solving)

### 3.2 SME Validation (Phase 1 Process)

**Conducted:** May 10, 2026  
**Raters:** 5 subject matter experts
- 2 AESOP course leads (AI Governance, Building with AI)
- 2 industry professionals (AI Engineer, Data Scientist)
- 1 academic researcher (AI Ethics)

**Validation Questions:**
1. "Do these O*NET codes accurately represent roles where this skill is critical?"
2. "Is this skill's WEF categorization appropriate and forward-looking?"
3. "Are any important occupational roles or skill categories missing?"

**Results:**
- 26 of 28 skills validated with "high confidence"
- 2 skills flagged for refinement (both rated "medium confidence")
  - `design-knowledge-graphs` — raters noted emerging tool landscape
  - `implement-safety-measures` — raters noted rapidly evolving safety standards
- No major gaps or missing occupational roles identified

### 3.3 Confidence Levels

**High Confidence (26 skills):** SMEs agree the O*NET/WEF mapping is appropriate and stable

**Medium Confidence (2 skills):** Raters noted uncertainty due to:
- Rapidly evolving tooling and standards
- Limited existing O*NET data for emerging roles
- Recommendation: Re-validate annually as tools and standards solidify

## 4. Key Insights & Implications

### 4.1 Job Market Relevance

**Finding:** AESOP's taxonomy aligns with real labor market demand as evidenced by:
- O*NET occupations showing strong growth (Data Scientists, Software Developers, Security Analysts)
- WEF identifying AI, critical thinking, and analytical skills as top 10 for 2024-2028
- Emerging roles (AI Prompt Engineers) appearing in O*NET 2024 updates

**Implication:** AESOP learners completing this taxonomy will have skills directly applicable to in-demand occupations.

### 4.2 Portability & Credibility

**Finding:** By aligning to O*NET and WEF frameworks, AESOP skills become:
- **Recognizable to employers** using O*NET for job classification
- **Comparable to other certifications** that also reference these frameworks
- **Portable across sectors** (skills map to occupations across industries)

**Implication:** Students can cite AESOP skill mastery as evidence of meeting WEF/O*NET competency frameworks on resumes and in job applications.

### 4.3 Emerging Skill Gaps

**Findings from O*NET analysis:**
- Prompt Engineering appears in ~2024 O*NET updates but limited historical data
- AI safety and alignment skills are emerging; limited O*NET specification yet
- RAG and vector database skills are new; not yet standardized in occupational frameworks

**Implication:** AESOP is positioning learners ahead of formal labor market classifications, capturing skills before they become universally required.

### 4.4 Future-Proofing Strategy

WEF emphasizes that 39% of skills will change by 2030. AESOP addresses this through:

1. **Foundational cognitive skills** (critical thinking, analytical thinking) that transcend specific technologies
2. **Responsible AI emphasis** (ethics, bias, safety) likely to become regulatory requirements
3. **Adaptability** (navigate change, lifelong learning) built into multiple skill definitions
4. **Communication** (explain AI to diverse audiences) across skill domains

## 5. Recommendations for Implementation

### 5.1 Messaging & Marketing

- Highlight O*NET and WEF alignment in course materials
- Show students the occupations and career paths their skills enable
- Reference specific O*NET job zones and WEF skill rankings in marketing

### 5.2 Assessment Design (Phase 2)

- When developing assessment items, reference O*NET work activities
- Ensure items assess WEF-priority skills (critical thinking, analytical thinking, creativity)
- Build in assessment of "soft" skills emphasized by WEF (communication, collaboration)

### 5.3 Student Reporting (Phase 4)

- Provide students with:
  - Numeric skill scores (0-100)
  - Proficiency level (Novice, Developing, Proficient, Advanced)
  - **O*NET occupational context** (e.g., "This skill is critical in Data Scientist roles")
  - **WEF skill category** (e.g., "Aligns with Analytical Thinking, a top 5 WEF skill")
  - Career pathway suggestions

### 5.4 Annual Re-Validation

- Monitor O*NET updates (released annually in Q3)
- Track WEF Future of Jobs reports (updated annually)
- Annually validate AESOP taxonomy against emerging frameworks
- Update skill definitions if significant shifts occur
